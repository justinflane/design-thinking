Journey Mapping

Journey mapping is an ethnographic approach that aims to uncover insights into peoples lives through close observation in order to explore their current reality.

Journey mapping is typically used in the Inspiration phase of the design thinking process. By observing people as they move through a space and/or use a product or service, you can gain fresh insights into why and how people do what they do.

These insights, viewed with empathy, help to really understand peoples motivations and experiences.

Brainstorming

Brainstorming occurs during the Ideation phase of the design thinking process. It is an opportunity for collaboration and creativity to flourish.

There are many brainstorming techniques available, however, the basic idea is generate as many ideas as possible that could potentially solve your defined problem/challenge.

The design and innovation company Ideo suggests that brainstorming sessions should be governed by 7 basic rules

  • > Encourage wild ideas
  • > Be visual
  • > Build on the ideas of others
  • > Stay focused on the topic
  • > Defer judgment
  • > Go for quantity
  • > One conversation at a time

Value Proposition Canvas

The value proposition canvas is a tool developed by Strategyzer that assists in designing products or services that customers actually want.

It works by identifying your customers job to be done, and the gains and pains associated with undertaking that job (right side of canvas).

The next step is to match your product or service so that it relieves pains and increases the gains for the customer. Thus increasing the value of your solution for the customer (left side of the canvas).

Mind Map

Mind mapping is a visual method to help organise ideas.

A mind map starts with one idea in the center, then other ideas, thoughts, words and images branch off.

Using a mind map can help to identify patterns or common themes in your collected data.

Growth Mindset

Design thinking is about empathy, putting yourself in someone else's shoes to understand how they experience something. Creating solutions for others without empathy is simply assuming your ideas will fix their problems.

Stepping outside of your own assumptions is important. Having the ability to recognise that you don't have all the answers straight away, and being comfortable with the uncertainity this creates is a neccessary element for creativity and innovation to occur.

A growth mindset means to adopt the idea that learning occurs when we step away from the familiar. With a growth mindset uncertainty and failure become important tools for learning, and learning is an esential part to improving solutions.

Prototyping

Prototyping involes creating scaled down versions of your solution to test with real users.

A protoytped solution is typically refered to as an MVP, Minimum Viable Product. Basically the cheapest, smallest version of your solution that contains just enough features to test and experiment with.

Testing an MVP with real users allows you to gather feedback. This feedback can then be learnt from so that your MVP can be improved and tested again.

After multiple rounds of rapid protoyping you will have gained enough feedback to create a fully functioning solution.

Importantly, the solution, having gone through this process, will be tried and tested before going to market, therefore greatly reducing the risk associated with launching a new product or service.
