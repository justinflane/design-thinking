What is Design Thinking

Design Thinking Introduction

Design thinking is a human centered approach to solving problems and challenges for real people. It starts with, and requires a deep understanding and empathy of people to uncover their unmet needs.

Design thinking does not expect new ideas to magically appear out of thin air. Rather, by observing and understanding human centered problems and experiences, innovative new solutions can be created.

These solutions are tested and itereated upon so that there is no need to rely on untested assumptions to create new products, services or business models.

The design thinking process is a collaborative effort that inspires creativity and innovation, which can be applied in any organisation.

When you need to acheive a breakthough innovation or make a creative leap, design thinking can help you dive into the problem and find new insights.

Design Thinking Process

  • Inspiration
  • Synthesis
  • Ideation
  • Implementation

Inspiration

The Inspiration phase of the design thinking process is a poweful way to uncover unmet needs. By observing peoples behaviour, understanding their motivations and how they experience things in context can help to trigger insights.

Rather than simply asking people "what they want", ask and experience what they do and what their world looks like.

Inspiration is the process of discovering an opportunity that motivates the search for solutions.

Synthesis

Synthesising your collected insights is an important step in the process. The aim of this phase is to make sense of the observations, insights and unmet needs you have uncovered.

Synthesis involves looking for patterns and identifying common themes. Once these have become clear you can then reframe the problem you are trying to solve into and actionable statement and focus your attention on it.

An actionable statement begins with "How might we...".

Ideation

The ideation phase is where we start to use creativity to come up with innovative solutions for the problem we have identified and defined in the synthesis phase.

In the ideation phase we aim to generate as many ideas as possible that might help to solve our problem.

The most promising ideas are selected and put to the test in iterative rounds of rapid protyping to understand what works, and what doesn't. Based on user feedback, this process continues until the solution is ready for implementation.

Ideation involes generating numerous ideas. Crazy ideas should be encouraged!

Implementation

The implementation phase happens only when the finished solution is ready for market.

Some may say a product or service is never truly finished. This is fine as it is good practice to continually learn and evolve solutions.

Having a beta mindset allows you to continually learn and improve your solution.
